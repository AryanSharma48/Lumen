# Unit Economics & Financial Viability

### 1. The Value of a Converted Lead (LTV)
A converted lead is worth **$1,200 in gross margin** to Credex.
* **Reasoning:** The target Pre-Series A startup burns roughly $4,000/month on decentralized AI subscriptions and API calls. Our audit engine conservatively identifies 20% waste (overlapping seats, inefficient API routing), finding $800/month in savings. Over a 12-month contract, that is $9,600 in optimized infrastructure. Assuming Credex negotiates the consolidated enterprise tiers and takes a standard 12.5% broker margin/success fee on the managed volume, Credex nets ~$1,200 per converted startup annually.

### 2. CAC by GTM Channel
Because our GTM relies on unscalable outbound rather than paid ads, our Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) is measured in SDR (Sales Development Rep) time. Assuming an SDR costs $40/hour:
* **Targeted GitHub Repo Scanning:** An SDR spends 2 hours ($80) scraping 100 relevant GitHub repos, finding LinkedIn profiles, and sending DMs. With a 10% conversion rate to the free tool, we get 10 audits. **CAC per Audit: $8.00.**
* **Fractional CFO Trojan Horse:** An SDR spends 3 hours ($120) pitching 50 Fractional CFOs on LinkedIn. 5 CFOs adopt the tool and mandate it for their 4 portfolio clients, yielding 20 audits. **CAC per Audit: $6.00.**
* **ACM Pre-Incubation Pipeline (The Unfair Advantage):** I leverage my existing Technical Head position to distribute the tool to 100 student founders at a 1-hour workshop ($0 SDR cost, zero marginal cost). **CAC per Audit: $0.00.**
* **Blended CAC per Audit:** Conservatively, **$5.00**.

### 3. Profitable Conversion Rates
To maintain an elite SaaS LTV:CAC ratio of 3:1 on a $1,200 LTV, our maximum allowable cost to acquire a *paying* customer (not just an audit) is $400. 

If generating one audit costs $5.00, we can afford to run 80 free audits to acquire one paying Credex customer ($400 / $5). Therefore, the end-to-end conversion funnel must achieve at least a **1.25% conversion rate** (1 closed deal per 80 audits) to remain highly profitable.

**The Target Funnel:**
* **Audit Completed -> Consultation Booked:** 10% (8 calls generated per 80 audits)
* **Consultation Booked -> Credit Purchase:** 12.5% (1 closed deal per 8 calls)
* **Net End-to-End Conversion:** 1.25% 

### 4. Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months
To generate $1,000,000 in ARR within 18 months using a $1,200 LTV, Credex needs **834 closed customers**. 

At our profitable baseline conversion rate of 1.25%, we must process **66,720 completed audits** over 18 months (547 days). 

| Metric | Target |
| :--- | :--- |
| **ARR Target** | $1,000,000 |
| **Required Customers** | 834 |
| **Total Audits Required** | 66,720 |
| **Required Daily Audits** | 122 |
| **Max Allowable Daily CAC Spend** | $610 |

**What must be true to achieve this?**
Generating 122 audits per day manually is impossible for a lean SDR team. For this to be true, the **viral coefficient (K-factor) must exceed 0.5**. The dynamic sharing route (`/r/[id]`) must successfully compel at least half of all engineering managers who complete an audit to share their financial report link in their private Slack channels, shifting the acquisition engine from linear SDR outbound to exponential product-led growth.
